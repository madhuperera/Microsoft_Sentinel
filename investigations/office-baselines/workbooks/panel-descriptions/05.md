### 🚩 05. People worth a closer look

| Workbook context | Value |
|---|---|
| Selected period | `{W_TimeRange:start}` to `{W_TimeRange:end}` |
| Baseline used for comparison | The immediately previous period of the same length |
| Normal change band | `+/- {W_NormalBandPct}%` |
| Target user filter | `{W_TargetUser}` *(blank means all users)* |

This is the short list. It shows only the people whose active-day pattern moved outside the normal change band compared with their own history, and only where there was enough past activity to be confident. People with steady activity are not shown.

This is a review indicator only, a prompt for a conversation in business context. It is not a productivity score and not a confirmed finding.

#### Column guide

`B_` means **Baseline** (the previous period). `C_` means **Current** (the selected period).

| Column | What it means |
|---|---|
| `UserDisplayName` | The person's display name. |
| `UPN` | The user principal name, usually their sign-in address. |
| `Direction` | Whether the change was an increase or a decrease (see below). |
| `ChangePct` | The percentage change in active days versus baseline. The sign shows the direction and the size shows how big the change is. |
| `B_ActiveDayRatio` | How often they were active in the baseline period. |
| `C_ActiveDayRatio` | How often they were active in the selected period. |
| `B_Events` | Total activity events in the baseline period. |
| `C_Events` | Total activity events in the selected period. |

#### Direction guide

| `Direction` | Meaning | Action |
|---|---|---|
| `Increase - review only` | More active than usual versus their baseline. | Review in business context. |
| `Decrease - review only` | Less active than usual versus their baseline. | Review in business context. |

#### What is included

Only people with a reliable baseline (enough past activity) whose change is beyond `+/- {W_NormalBandPct}%`. The same everyday staff activity and exclusions as the other views apply.

#### How rows are sorted

Rows show the largest change first, whether it is an increase or a decrease.
