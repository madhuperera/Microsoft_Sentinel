### 🏢 06. What "normal" looks like across the organisation

| Workbook context | Value |
|---|---|
| Selected period | `{W_TimeRange:start}` to `{W_TimeRange:end}` |
| Minimum activity to be counted | `{W_MinUserActivity}` events in the period |

This view shows the organisation's typical daily activity for each Microsoft 365 app. It is the yardstick used by the "each person compared with the organisation" view. It uses the median (the middle person) rather than the average, because the average is easily pulled up by a few very heavy users.

This is background context, not a league table.

#### Column guide

| Column | What it means |
|---|---|
| `Workload` | The Microsoft 365 app, for example Exchange, SharePoint, OneDrive or Microsoft Teams. |
| `Users` | How many people the figures are based on. Small numbers are less reliable. |
| `Median` | The organisation's typical events per day per person (the middle value, not the average). |
| `P25` | A lower-activity mark: a quarter of people are below this level. |
| `P75` | A higher-activity mark: a quarter of people are above this level. |
| `P90` | A high-activity mark: only about one in ten people are above this level. |
| `MAD` | How spread out activity is around the typical level. A larger value means people vary more. |

#### What is included

Everyday staff activity only. People below the minimum activity setting (`{W_MinUserActivity}` events) are left out, so very light or one-off accounts do not distort the typical level. Guests, system accounts and listed service accounts are excluded.

#### How rows are sorted

Rows are sorted by app name.
